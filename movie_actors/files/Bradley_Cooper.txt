'''Bradley Charles Cooper'''<ref>{{cite web|title=The Bradley Cooper Webchat Transcript|url=http://www.empireonline.com/interviews/interview.asp?IID=1217|publisher=Empire Online|accessdate=April 9, 2013}}</ref> (born January 5, 1975)<ref name=filmref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/16/Bradley-Cooper.html|title=Bradley Cooper biodata|publisher=FilmReference.com|accessdate=December 2, 2014}}</ref> is an American actor and producer. He has been nominated for four [[Academy Awards]], three for acting and one for producing, and one [[Tony Award]]. He was named the "[[Sexiest Man Alive]]" by ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' magazine in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bradley Cooper Is ''People''{{'}}s Sexiest Man Alive!|url=http://www.people.com/people/package/article/0,,20315920_20545580,00.html|work=People|accessdate=November 18, 2011|first=Alexis|last=Chiu|first2=Charlotte|last2=Triggs|date=November 16, 2011}}</ref>

Cooper has a [[B.A.]] in English from [[Georgetown University]] (1997) and an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] in acting from [[Actors Studio Drama School]] at [[The New School]] (2000). He first gained recognition in the spy-action television show ''[[Alias (TV series)|Alias]]'' and the television drama ''[[Jack & Bobby]]''. He later appeared in supporting roles in the romantic comedies ''[[Wedding Crashers]]'' (2005), ''[[Yes Man (film)|Yes Man]]'' (2008), and ''[[He's Just Not That Into You (film)|He's Just Not That Into You]]'' (2009). His breakthrough role came with the comedy franchise [[The Hangover (franchise)|''The Hangover'' trilogy]] (2009–13). Other notable films include the action-comedy ''[[The A-Team (film)|The A-Team]]'' (2010), the thriller ''[[Limitless]]'' (2011), the crime drama ''[[The Place Beyond the Pines]]'' (2013), and the superhero film ''[[Guardians of the Galaxy (film)|Guardians of the Galaxy]]'' (2014), in which he voiced [[Rocket Raccoon]].

Cooper garnered three consecutive Oscar nominations from 2013 to 2015 for his roles in the romantic comedy ''[[Silver Linings Playbook]]'' (2012), the comedy-drama ''[[American Hustle]]'' (2013), and the biographical war drama ''[[American Sniper (film)|American Sniper]]'' (2014). For producing ''American Sniper'', Cooper also received an [[Academy Award for Best Picture]] nomination. For starring in the 2014 [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] revival of the play ''[[The Elephant Man (play)|The Elephant Man]]'', Cooper garnered a [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play]] nomination.

==Career==

===1999–2008===
Cooper began his professional acting career with a small guest role in one episode of the television show ''[[Sex and the City]]'' in 1999<ref name=tca/> and served as a presenter in the travel-adventure series ''[[Globe Trekker]]''. After his film debut in the satirical comedy ''[[Wet Hot American Summer]]'', which later garnered a cult following, Cooper played [[Will Tippin]] in [[J.J. Abrams]]' successful television spy-action drama ''[[Alias (TV series)|Alias]]''<ref name=tca/> and acted in the 2002 psychological thriller ''[[Changing Lanes]]''. The scenes in which he appeared were edited out of the final cut of the film, but are featured on the film's DVD and Blu-ray releases.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/693650?view=cast |title=BFI Film & TV Database: ''Changing Lanes'' 2002 |publisher=[[British Film Institute]] |accessdate=August 28, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.slantmagazine.com/dvd/review/changing-lanes/9 |title=''Changing Lanes'' DVD Review |last=Gonzalez |first=Ed |work=[[Slant Magazine]]|date=August 27, 2002 |accessdate=August 28, 2011}}</ref> After leaving ''Alias'' in 2003, Cooper guest-starred on the short-lived TV series ''[[Miss Match]]'' and later returned twice to ''Alias'' to reprise his role in guest appearances.

Cooper played the part of cowboy and race horse trainer Morgan Murphy in the TV movie ''The Last Cowboy'', which aired on the [[Hallmark Channel]] in January, 2003. He also co-starred in the [[ABC Family]] film ''[[I Want to Marry Ryan Banks]]'' with [[Jason Priestley]], and appeared as a regular guest star in the [[WB Television Network|WB]] series ''[[Jack & Bobby]]''. He played the popular villain Sack Lodge in the hit comedy ''[[Wedding Crashers]]'' and appeared in the film ''[[Failure to Launch]]'' as Demo, a friend of Tripp ([[Matthew McConaughey]]). 
[[File:Bradley Cooper 2009.jpg|thumb|upright|Cooper at the 2009 [[Tribeca Film Festival]] premiere of [[Woody Allen]]'s film, ''[[Whatever Works]]'' in April 2009.]]

In September 2005, [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] debuted the sitcom ''[[Kitchen Confidential (TV series)|Kitchen Confidential]]'', based on a memoir by chef [[Anthony Bourdain]], with Cooper in the leading role. Jacobs Theater.<ref>{{cite news|last=Brantley |first=Ben |title=It's Her! It's Her! And, Oh Yeah, There's a Play |work=[[The New York Times]]|date=April 20, 2006}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Paul_Rudd_and_Bradley_Cooper_Round_out_Three_Days_of_Rain_Cast_with_Julia_Roberts_Show_Begins_March_28_20051129|title=Paul Rudd and Bradley Cooper Round out Three Days of Rain Cast with Julia Roberts, Show Begins March 28|work=Broadway World.com|date=November 29, 2005|accessdate=July 16, 2010}}</ref> He also appeared on stage as Jake in the 2008 production of [[Theresa Rebeck]]'s play ''The Understudy'' at the [[Williamstown Theatre Festival]] alongside [[Kristen Johnston]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/theater_arts/articles/2008/07/29/the_understudy_combines_comedy_and_kafka_in_williamstown/ |title={{-'}}The Understudy' combines comedy and Kafka in Williamstown|last=Kennedy |first=Louise |date=July 28, 2008 |accessdate=August 29, 2011| work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> Cooper appeared in Season 5 of ''[[Nip/Tuck]]'' (2007) as Aidan Stone, a television star on the fictional show ''Hearts 'N Scalpels''. ===2009–2013===

On February 7, 2009, Cooper hosted ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' with musical guest [[TV on the Radio]].  In one skit, Cooper portrayed actor [[Christian Bale]] as spokesperson for a DVD called, "No, Bruce! Let Me Finish! The Best of Celebrity Tirades." 
In 2009, Cooper starred in the films ''[[He's Just Not That Into You (film)|He's Just Not That Into You]]'', ''[[Case 39]]'',<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20297504,00.html |title=Bradley, Renée Get Cozy in Barcelona |work=People |date=August 12, 2009 |accessdate=July 16, 2010}}</ref> ''[[All About Steve (film)|All About Steve]]'' and ''[[The Hangover (film)|The Hangover]]'', which was wildly successful. Cooper's role as [[Phil Wenneck]] in ''The Hangover'' is perhaps the role for which he is best known. The film first opened in the United States and went on to gross over $467 million worldwide. Its production budget was $35 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=hangover.htm|title=The Hangover (2009)|work=[[Box Office Mojo]]|publisher=[[Internet Movie Database]]|accessdate=January 8, 2013}}</ref> Following ''The Hangover'', the premiere of ''All About Steve'' was met with mostly negative reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/all_about_steve[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|title=All About Steve|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> Cooper's performance, along with co-star Sandra Bullock, was poorly received by critics and, despite his recent success with ''[[The Hangover (film)|The Hangover]]'', the film only saw mild success at the box office.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?page=main&id=allaboutsteve.htm[[Box Office Mojo]]|title=All About Steve|publisher=[[Box Office Mojo]]|accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>  Although the success of ''The Hangover'' brought Cooper new opportunities, Cooper stated in a 2011 interview with [[Shave (magazine)|Shave Magazine]], "It’s the same. From late 2014 through early 2015, Cooper starred on Broadway in ''[[The Elephant Man (play)|The Elephant Man]]'', directed by Scott Ellis, for which he garnered a [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play]] nomination.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/tony-awards-nominations-announced-791965|title=Tony Awards Nominations Announced|work=Hollywood Reporter|date=April 28, 2015|accessdate=April 28, 2015}}</ref>

In March 2012, Cooper and [[Warner Bros.]] entered into a two-year [[first look deal]] for his production company, 22 & Indiana Pictures.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bradley Cooper pacts with Warner Bros.|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118050943|accessdate=March 3, 2012|newspaper=Variety|date=March 1, 2012|author=Dave McNary|author2=Jeff Sneider}}</ref> In 2014, Cooper and director [[Todd Phillips]], who directed him in ''[[The Hangover (film series)|The Hangover]]'' signed a three-year first-look deal with Warner Bros.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thewrap.com/bradley-cooper-todd-phillips-form-warner-bros-based-production-company/ |title=Bradley Cooper, Todd Phillips Merge Production Companies at Warner Bros. |publisher=''The Wrap'' |date=May 16, 2014 |author=Lucas Shaw |accessdate=January 7, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://variety.com/2014/film/news/bradley-cooper-and-todd-phillips-form-new-production-company-exclusive-1201181329/ |title=Bradley Cooper and Todd Phillips Form New Production Company (EXCLUSIVE) |publisher=''Variety'' |date=May 16, 2014 |first=Justin |last=Kroll |accessdate=January 7, 2015 }}</ref>

Cooper does not drink alcohol, from which he [[Teetotalism|has abstained]] since he was 29, saying that it would have "sabotaged" his life.<ref name="thr2012">{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/bradley-cooper-evolution-leading-man-the-hangover-367781 |title=Bradley Cooper: The Serious Evolution of a Leading Man|publisher=''The Hollywood Reporter''|date=September 5, 2012|first=Stephen|last=Galloway|accessdate=November 18, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/woman/celeb-news/cooper-booze-would-have-ruined-me-29849280.html|title=Cooper: Booze would have ruined me|date=December 18, 2013|work=Irish Independent|accessdate=December 18, 2013}}</ref>

Cooper married actress [[Jennifer Esposito]] in late 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20006812,00.html|title=Bradley Cooper and Jennifer Esposito Marry|first=Julie|last=Jordan|work=People|date=January 4, 2007|accessdate=July 16, 2010}}</ref> In May 2007, it was announced that Esposito had filed for divorce.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20037538,00.html|title=Jennifer Esposito, Bradley Cooper Split|first=Emily|last=Fromm|work=People|date=May 2, 2007|accessdate=July 16, 2010}}</ref> Cooper was in a relationship with [[Renée Zellweger]] from 2009 to 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bradley Cooper Talks Dating Renee Zellweger, Losing Virginity|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/03/16/bradley-cooper-talks-dati_n_836391.html|accessdate=July 14, 2003|publisher=Huffington Post|date=March 16, 2011|first=Jordan|last=Zakarin}}</ref> He dated [[Zoe Saldana]] from December 2011 to January 2013.<ref>{{Cite news|date=September 25, 2012|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/bradley-cooper-zoe-saldana-dating-again-2012259|title=Bradley Cooper, Zoe Saldana Dating Again!|work=[[Us Weekly]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=January 3, 2013|url= http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20661141,00.html|title=Buzz:Bradley Cooper & Zoe Saldana Split Again|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref> He then dated model-actress [[Suki Waterhouse]], with whom he was first seen publicly starting in March 2013; they attended the Academy Awards together in February 2015, but by the following month media reports said they had broken up.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.eonline.com/news/637477/bradley-cooper-and-suki-waterhouse-break-up| title=News/ Bradley Cooper and Suki Waterhouse Break Up| first=Marc|last= Malkin|publisher= [[E!
