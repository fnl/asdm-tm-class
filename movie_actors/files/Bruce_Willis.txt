'''Walter Bruce Willis''' (born March 19, 1955) is a German-born American actor, producer and singer. His career began on the [[Off-Broadway]] stage and then in television in the 1980s, most notably as David Addison in ''[[Moonlighting (TV series)|Moonlighting]]'' (1985–89). He is perhaps best known for his role of [[John McClane]] in the [[Die Hard (franchise)|''Die Hard'' series]], which were mostly critical and uniformly financial successes.  He has appeared in over sixty films, including ''[[Color of Night]]'' (1994), ''[[Pulp Fiction]]'' (1994), ''[[12 Monkeys]]'' (1995), ''[[The Fifth Element]]'' (1997), ''[[Armageddon (1998 film)|Armageddon]]'' (1998), ''[[The Sixth Sense]]'' (1999), ''[[Unbreakable (film)|Unbreakable]]'' (2000), ''[[Sin City (film)|Sin City]]'' (2005), ''[[Red (2010 film)|Red]]'' (2010) and ''[[The Expendables 2]]'' (2012).

Motion pictures featuring Willis have [[Gross (economics)|grossed]] US$2.64 billion to $3.05 billion at North American box offices, making him the eighth highest-grossing actor in a leading role and 12th-highest including supporting roles.<ref name="BOMlist">{{cite web|publisher=[[Box Office Mojo]]|title=People Index|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/?view=Actor&sort=sumgross&p=.htm|accessdate=August 29, 2010}}</ref><ref name="The Numbers">{{cite web|title=All Time Top 100 Stars at the Box Office|publisher=The Numbers|url=http://www.the-numbers.com/people/records/|accessdate=August 29, 2010}}</ref> He is a two-time [[Emmy Award]]–winning, [[Golden Globe Award]]–winning and four-time [[Saturn Award]]–nominated actor. Willis was married to actress [[Demi Moore]] and they had three daughters before their divorce in 2000, following thirteen years of marriage. Since 2009, he has been married to model [[Emma Heming]], with whom he has two daughters.

== Early life ==
Willis was born on March 19, 1955, in [[Idar-Oberstein]], [[West Germany]].<ref name="bbcnews" /><ref name="DailyM" /> His father, David Willis, was an [[United States|American]] soldier.  His mother, Marlene K.,<ref>{{cite news | authorlink= Army Archerd | last= Archerd | first=Army | date= December 11, 2003 | url = http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117897071?refCatId=1000 |title= Inside Move: Flu KOs Smart Set yule bash | work= [[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | accessdate= November 14, 2011}}</ref> was [[Germans|German]], born in [[Kassel, Germany|Kassel]].<ref name="bbcnews">{{cite news|title=Surprise German visit from Willis|publisher=BBC News|date=August 8, 2005 |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/film/4132632.stm|accessdate=May 9, 2009|quote=His mother Marlene was born in the nearby town of Kassel.}}</ref><ref name="DailyM">{{cite news|last=Lipworth|first=Elaine|title=Die Another Day: Bruce Willis|work=Daily Mail |location=UK|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/pages/live/articles/live/live.html?in_article_id=462215&in_page_id=1889|date=June 16, 2007|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> Willis is the oldest of four children: he has a sister, Florence, and a brother, David. His brother Robert died of [[pancreatic cancer]] in 2001, aged 42.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117802168|title=Robert Willis Obituary|date=July 1, 2001|accessdate=June 23, 2011|work=Variety }}</ref> After being discharged from the military in 1957, Willis's father took his family back to [[Carneys Point Township, New Jersey]].<ref name=tca>Stated on ''[[Inside the Actors Studio]]'', 2001</ref> Willis has described himself as having come from a "long line of blue collar people";<ref name=tca/> his mother worked in a bank and his father was a welder, master mechanic, and factory worker.<ref name="BioSarah">{{cite web|last=Barnard|first=Sarah|title=Bruce Willis|publisher=[[The Biography Channel]]|url=http://94.236.123.155:8082/biographies/bruce-willis.html|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20110531143427/http://94.236.123.155:8082/biographies/bruce-willis.html|archivedate=May 31, 2011|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> Willis attended [[Penns Grove High School]] in his hometown, where he encountered issues with a [[stuttering|stutter]].<ref name=tca/> He was nicknamed ''Buck-Buck'' by his schoolmates.<ref name="BioSarah" /><ref name="NYTPennHS">{{cite news|last=Petersen|first=Melody|title=Bruce Willis Drops Project, Leaving Town More Troubled|work=The New York Times|date=May 9, 1997 |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9A02E6DF1439F93AA35756C0A961958260 |accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref><ref name="BWillisUncut">{{cite news|work=[[Reader's Digest]]|title=Bruce Willis: The Uncut Interview |url=http://www.rd.com/images/content/021102/bruce_willis_interview.pdf|year=2002 |format=PDF |accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> Finding it easy to express himself on stage and losing his stutter in the process, Willis began performing on stage; and his high school activities were marked by such things as the drama club and [[student council]] president.<ref name="BioSarah" />

After high school, Willis took a job as a security guard at the [[Salem Nuclear Power Plant]]<ref name="DailyShow07">{{cite episode |title=Bruce Willis |series=[[The Daily Show]] |network=[[Comedy Central]] |station= |airdate=June 26, 2007 |url=http://www.thedailyshow.com/watch/tue-june-26-2007/bruce-willis}}</ref><ref name="WashPostTragic" /> and transported work crews at the [[DuPont]] Chambers Works factory in [[Deepwater, New Jersey]].<ref name="WashPostTragic">{{cite news|last=Segal|first=David|title=Bruce Willis's Tragic Mask|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=March 10, 2005|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A22175-2005Mar9.html|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref>

After working as a [[private investigator]] (a role he would play in the television series ''[[Moonlighting (TV series)|Moonlighting]]'' as well as in the 1991 film, ''[[The Last Boy Scout]]''), Willis turned to acting. He enrolled in the Drama Program at [[Montclair State University]], where he was cast in the class production of ''[[Cat on a Hot Tin Roof]]''. Willis left school in his junior year and moved to [[New York City]],<ref name="DailyM" /> where in the early 1980s he supported himself as a bartender at the West 19th Street art bar Kamikaze.<ref>{{cite book | last = Curley | first=Mallory | title = A [[Cookie Mueller]] Encyclopedia | publisher = Randy Press | year = 2010 | page = 260}}</ref>

After multiple auditions, Willis made his theater debut in the [[Off-Broadway]] production of ''Heaven and Earth''. He gained more experience and exposure in ''[[Fool for Love (play)|Fool for Love]]'', and in a [[Levi's]] commercial. Willis also played a lead role in the Off-Broadway production of writer-director Dennis Watlington's ''Bullpen'' for four years.{{citation needed|date=December 2013}}

== Career ==

=== Early career ===
[[File:Bruce Willis - 1987.jpg|thumb|Willis upon receiving an Emmy Award in 1987 for Best Actor in [[Moonlighting (TV series)|''Moonlighting'']]]]
Willis left New York City and headed to [[California]] to audition for several television shows.<ref name="DailyM" /> In 1984, he appeared in an episode of the TV series ''[[Miami Vice]]'', titled "[[No Exit (Miami Vice)|No Exit]]". In 1985, he was the guest actor in the first episode of the 1980s revival of ''[[The Twilight Zone (1985 TV series)|The Twilight Zone]]'', "[[Shatterday (The Twilight Zone)|Shatterday]]".<ref>{{IMDb name|246}}</ref> He auditioned for the role of David Addison Jr. of the television series ''[[Moonlighting (TV series)|Moonlighting]]'' (1985–89), competing against 3,000 other actors for the position.<ref name="YahooM">{{cite web|title=Yahoo! Movies|work=Bruce Willis Biography |url=http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800018749/bio |accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> The starring role, opposite [[Cybill Shepherd]], helped to establish him as a comedic actor, with the show lasting five seasons.<ref name=tca/> During the height of the show's success, beverage maker [[Seagram]] hired Willis as the pitchman for their Golden Wine Cooler products.<ref name="WillisCool">{{cite news |title=How Bruce Willis Keeps His Cool |work=Time |url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1635812,00.html |date=June 21, 2007 |accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> The advertising campaign paid the rising star between $5–7 million over two years. In spite of that, Willis chose not to renew his contract with the company when he decided to stop drinking alcohol in 1988.<ref name="Playboy">{{cite news|last=Grobel|first=Lawrence|title=Playboy Interview: Bruce Willis|work=[[Playboy]]|pages=59–79|date=November 1988}}</ref>

Willis had his first starring role in a feature film in the 1987 [[Blake Edwards]] film ''[[Blind Date (1987 film)|Blind Date]]'', with [[Kim Basinger]] and [[John Larroquette]].<ref name=tca/> Edwards cast him again to play the real-life cowboy actor [[Tom Mix]] in ''[[Sunset (film)|Sunset]]'' (1988). However, it was his then-unexpected turn in the film ''[[Die Hard]]'' (1988) as [[John McClane]] that catapulted him to movie star status.<ref name=tca/> He performed most of his own [[stunt]]s in the film,<ref name="People1">{{cite web|title=Bruce Willis: Biography|work=[[People (American magazine)|People]] |url=http://www.people.com/people/bruce_willis/biography|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> and the film grossed $138,708,852 worldwide.<ref name="BOM1">{{cite web|publisher=[[Box Office Mojo]]|title=Die Hard|url=http://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=diehard.htm|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> Following his success with ''Die Hard'', he had a supporting role in the drama ''[[In Country]]'' as Vietnam veteran Emmett Smith and also provided the voice for a talking baby in ''[[Look Who's Talking]]'', as well as its sequel ''[[Look Who's Talking Too]]''.

In the late 1980s, Willis enjoyed moderate success as a [[recording artist]], recording an album of pop-blues titled ''[[The Return of Bruno (album)|The Return of Bruno]]'', which included the hit single "[[Respect Yourself]]",<ref name="TopSongs">{{cite web|title=Top 100 Songs of 1987|publisher=The Eighties Club|url=http://eightiesclub.tripod.com/id224.htm|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> promoted by a [[This Is Spinal Tap|Spinal Tap]]–like [[rockumentary]] [[parody]] featuring scenes of him performing at famous events including [[Woodstock]]. He released a version of the [[The Drifters|Drifters]] song "[[Under The Boardwalk]]" as a follow-up, which got to No. 2 in the [[UK Top 40]], though was less successful in the U.S. Willis returned to the recording studio several times afterward. (See Discography below.)

=== 1990s ===
Having acquired major personal success and pop culture influence playing John McClane in ''Die Hard'', Willis reprised his role in the sequels ''[[Die Hard 2]]'' (1990) and ''[[Die Hard with a Vengeance]]'' (1995).<ref name=tca/> These first three installments in the ''[[Die Hard (franchise)|Die Hard]]'' series grossed over US$700 million internationally and propelled Willis to the first rank of Hollywood action stars.

In the early 1990s, Willis's career suffered a moderate slump starring in [[Box office bomb|flops]], such as ''[[The Bonfire of the Vanities (film)|The Bonfire of the Vanities]]'', ''[[Striking Distance]]'' and a film he co-wrote titled ''[[Hudson Hawk]]'', among others. He starred in a leading role in the highly sexualized [[erotic thriller]], ''[[Color of Night]]'' (1994): another box office failure, it was savaged by critics but did well in the [[home video]] market and became one of the Top 20 most-rented films in the United States in 1995.<ref>''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' vol 108 No. 1 (1/6/1996) p.54.</ref>

In 1994, he had a supporting role in [[Quentin Tarantino]]'s acclaimed ''[[Pulp Fiction (film)|Pulp Fiction]]'',<ref name=tca/> which gave a new boost to his career. In 1996, he was the executive producer of the [[cartoon]] ''[[Bruno the Kid]]'' which featured a [[Computer-generated imagery|CGI]] representation of himself.<ref name="FilmRef">{{cite web|title=Bruce Willis Biography (1955–)|publisher=Filmreference|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/99/Bruce-Willis.html|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> He went on to play the lead roles in ''[[Twelve Monkeys]]'' (1995) and ''[[The Fifth Element]]'' (1997). However, by the end of the 1990s, his career had fallen into another slump with critically panned films like ''[[The Jackal (1997 film)|The Jackal]]'', ''[[Mercury Rising]]'', and ''[[Breakfast of Champions (film)|Breakfast of Champions]]'', saved only by the success of the [[Michael Bay]]-directed ''[[Armageddon (1998 film)|Armageddon]]'' which was the highest grossing film of 1998 worldwide.<ref name="BOM2">{{cite web|title=1998 Worldwide Grosses|publisher=[[Box Office Mojo]]|url=http://boxofficemojo.com/yearly/chart/?view2=worldwide&yr=1998&p=.htm|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> The same year his voice and likeness were featured in the [[PlayStation]] video game ''[[Apocalypse (video game)|Apocalypse]]''.<ref name="EWeekly">{{cite news|last=Walk|first=Gary Eng|title="Apocalypse" Now|work=Entertainment Weekly |url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,286028,00.html|date=December 4, 1998 |accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> In 1999, Willis then went on to the starring role in [[M. Night Shyamalan]]'s film, ''[[The Sixth Sense]]''. The film was both a commercial and critical success<ref name=tca/> and helped to increase interest in his acting career.

== Business activities ==
Willis owns property in Los Angeles and in [[Penns Grove, New Jersey]]; rents apartments at [[Trump Tower (New York City)|Trump Tower]]<ref name="trump">{{cite web|title=Bruce Willis Moves Into Trump Towers|publisher=SoFeminine.ca|url=http://www.sofeminine.co.uk/w/star/n117308/news/Bruce-Willis-Moves-Into-Trump-Towers.html|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> and in [[Riverside South, Manhattan]],<ref name="TrumpPlace">{{cite news|last=Abelson|first=Max|title=Bruce Willis Pays $4.26 M. for Trump Enemy's Condo|publisher=[[The New York Observer]] |url=http://www.observer.com/2007/bruce-willis-pays-4-26-m-trump-enemys-condo|date=November 5, 2007|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> both in New York City; has a home in [[Malibu, California]]; a ranch in [[Montana]]; a beach home on [[Parrot Cay]] in [[Turks and Caicos]]; and multiple properties in [[Sun Valley, Idaho]].<ref name="DailyM" />

In 2000, Willis, with his business partner Arnold Rifkin, started a motion picture production company called [[Cheyenne Enterprises]]. He left the company to be run solely by Rifkin in 2007 after ''Live Free or Die Hard''.<ref name="Holly2">{{cite news|last=Fleming|first=Michael|title=Willis held 'Hostage'|work=Variety |url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-26789071_ITM |registration=yes|date=November 12, 2002|accessdate=May 10, 2009}} {{Dead link|date=April 2012|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> He also owns several small businesses in [[Hailey, Idaho]], including The Mint Bar and The Liberty Theater and is a co-founder of [[Planet Hollywood]], with actors [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] and [[Sylvester Stallone]].<ref name="PlanetH">{{cite news|last=Martinson|first=Jane|author2=Vikram Dodd|title=Planet Hollywood crashes to earth |work=The Guardian |location=London |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/uk/1999/aug/18/janemartinson.vikramdodd |date=August 18, 1999|accessdate=May 10, 2009 }}</ref> In 2009 Willis signed a contract to become the international face of [[Belvédère (company)|Belvedere SA]]'s [[Sobieski Vodka]] in exchange for 3.3% ownership in the company.<ref>{{cite news |author=Baubeau, Amelie and David Kesmodel |title=Bruce Willis Sees Spirits in Equity Deal With Belvedere |url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748703478704574611690552812758.html |work=The Wall Street Journal |date=December 23, 2009 }}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Willis' acting role models are [[Gary Cooper]], [[Robert De Niro]], [[Steve McQueen]] and [[John Wayne]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.biography.com/articles/Bruce-Willis-9533244|publisher=biography.com|title=Bruce Willis Biography|accessdate=June 7, 2010}}</ref>

=== Relationships and children ===
At the premiere for the film ''[[Stakeout (1987 film)|Stakeout]]'', Willis met actress [[Demi Moore]]. Willis married Moore on November 21, 1987 and had three daughters: [[Rumer Willis]] (b. August 16, 1988), Scout LaRue Willis (b. 1991) and Tallulah Belle Willis (b. 1994) before the couple divorced on October 18, 2000. The couple gave no public reason for their breakup. Regarding the divorce, Willis stated, "I felt I had failed as a father and a husband by not being able to make it work." He credited actor [[Will Smith]] for helping him cope with the situation.<ref name="DailyM" /><ref name="WillisCool" /> After their breakup, rumors persisted that the couple planned to remarry, until Demi Moore married [[Ashton Kutcher]]. Willis has maintained a close relationship with both Moore and Kutcher, even attending their wedding.

Willis was engaged to actress [[Brooke Burns]] until they broke up in 2004 after ten months together.<ref name="YahooM" /> He married model [[Emma Heming]] in [[Turks and Caicos]] on March 21, 2009;<ref name="">{{cite news|title=Bruce Willis Married to Super Model Emma heming|url=http://www.celebrityness.com/2009/05/bruce-willis-married-22-years-younger.html|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5msOjCyn4|archivedate=January 18, 2010|accessdate=April 11, 2009}}</ref> guests included his three daughters, Demi Moore, and Ashton Kutcher. The ceremony was not legally binding, so the couple wed again in a civil ceremony in [[Beverly Hills]], six days later.<ref name="MarriageHeming">{{cite news|title=Bruce and Emma make marriage legal|publisher=[[MSNBC]]|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/29923036/|date=March 27, 2009|accessdate=May 9, 2009}}</ref> The couple has two daughters: Mabel Ray Willis (b. 2012)<ref name="Mabel">{{cite news|title=It's a Girl for Bruce Willis and Emma Heming!|publisher=People|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20583731,00.html|date=April 2, 2012|accessdate=April 2, 2012}}</ref> and Evelyn Penn Willis (b. 2014).<ref>{{cite news|title=Bruce Willis' Wife Emma Heming-Willis Gives Birth, Couple Welcomes Second Girl, Baby Evelyn Penn|work=Us Weekly|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-moms/news/bruce-willis-wife-emma-heming-willis-gives-birth-to-second-girl-201475|date=May 7, 2014|accessdate=May 7, 2014}}</ref>

=== Religious views ===
[[File:Bruce willis cinedom.jpg|thumb|upright|Willis at the German premiere of ''[[Over the Hedge (film)|Over the Hedge]]'' on June 28, 2006]]
Willis was, at one point, [[Lutheranism|Lutheran]] (specifically [[Lutheran Church–Missouri Synod]]),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hope-elca.com/famous/ |title=Famous Lutherans |publisher=Hope-elca.com |accessdate=August 22, 2010}}{{dead link|date=June 2012}}</ref> but no longer practices. They were all very important when we didn't know why the sun moved, why weather changed, why hurricanes occurred, or volcanoes happened. ... Modern religion is the end trail of modern mythology. But there are people who interpret the Bible literally. Literally! I choose not to believe that's the way. And that's what makes America cool, you know?<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Tom |first1=Dunkel |date=July 1998 |title=Bruce Willis Kicks Asteroid in Armageddon |journal=[[George (magazine)|George]] |volume= |issue= |pages= |url=http://www.tomdunkel.com/portfolio/article.aspx?articleTitle=Bruce%20Willis%20Kicks%20Asteroid |accessdate=May 22, 2012 }} <!-- Cited from the author's Web site, though I can't find the volume, issue, or pages. --></ref>}}

== Political views ==
In 1988, Willis and then-wife Demi Moore campaigned for [[Massachusetts]] Governor [[Michael Dukakis]]'s Presidential bid. Four years later, he supported President [[George H. W. Bush]] for reelection and was an outspoken critic of [[Bill Clinton]]. However, in 1996, he declined to endorse Clinton's [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] opponent [[Bob Dole]], because Dole had criticized Demi Moore for her role in the film ''[[Striptease (film)|Striptease]]''.<ref name="DoleNo">{{cite news|last=Vincent|first=Mal|title=Playing the bad boy is a natural for Bruce Willis|publisher=HamptonRoads.com|url=http://hamptonroads.com/2006/03/playing-bad-boy-natural-bruce-willis|date=March 3, 2006|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> Willis was an invited speaker at the [[2000 Republican National Convention]],<ref name="CNNarchives">{{cite news|title=Bush and Cheney head toward Philadelphia as party vanguard makes preparations |publisher=CNN |url=http://archives.cnn.com/2000/ALLPOLITICS/stories/07/28/convention.wrap/index.html|date=July 28, 2000|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> and supported [[George W. Bush]] that year. He did not make any contributions or public endorsements in the [[United States presidential election, 2008|2008 presidential campaign]]. In several June 2007 interviews, he declared that he maintains some [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] ideologies.<ref name="DailyM" /><ref name="WillisCool" />

In 2006, he said that the United States should intervene more into [[Colombia]], in order to end the drug trafficking.<ref name="MSNBC1">{{cite news|last=Walls|first=Jeannette|title=Bruce Willis blasts Colombian drug trade |publisher=[[MSNBC]] |url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/11701188 |date=March 14, 2006|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> In several interviews Willis has said that he supports large salaries for teachers and police officers, and said he is disappointed in the United States [[foster care]] system as well as treatment of [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Native Americans]].<ref name="DoleNo" /><ref name="USAWeek1">{{cite news|last=West|first=Kevin|title=A Big Ride of a Life|work=[[USA Weekend]] |url=http://www.usaweekend.com/07_issues/070624/070624bruce_willis.html |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5msOk4Dax |archivedate=January 18, 2010 |date=June 24, 2007 |accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> Willis also stated that he is a supporter of gun rights, stating, "Everyone has a right to bear arms. If you take guns away from legal gun owners, then the only people who have guns are the bad guys."<ref name="USAWeek2">{{cite news |last=Roach |first=Mary|title=Being Bruce Willis|work=[[USA Weekend]] | url = http://www.usaweekend.com/00_issues/000213/000213willis.html |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5msOk5618 |archivedate=January 18, 2010 |date=February 13, 2000|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref>

In February 2006, Willis appeared in Manhattan to talk about his film ''[[16 Blocks]]'' with reporters. One reporter attempted to ask Willis about his opinion on the current government, but was interrupted by Willis in mid-sentence: "I'm sick of answering this fucking question. I'm a Republican only as far as I want a smaller government, I want less government intrusion. I want them to stop shitting on my money and your money and tax dollars that we give 50 percent of every year. I want them to be fiscally responsible and I want these goddamn lobbyists out of Washington. Do that and I'll say I'm a Republican. I hate the government, OK? I'm apolitical. Write that down. I'm not a Republican."<ref name="MSN Movies">{{cite web|title=Willis Is Mad As Hell...|publisher=MSN Movies|url=http://movies.msn.com/movies/hitlist/2-24-06?GT1=100|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20060425123801/http://movies.msn.com/movies/hitlist/2-24-06?GT1=100|archivedate=April 25, 2006|date=February 24, 2006|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref>

Willis's name was in an advertisement in the ''Los Angeles Times'' on August 17, 2006, that condemned [[Hamas]] and [[Hezbollah]] and supported [[Israel]] in the [[2006 Israel-Lebanon war]].<ref>[http://www.hollywoodgrind.com/nicole-kidman-and-84-others-stand-united-against-terrorism/ "Nicole Kidman and 84 Others Stand United Against Terrorism."] ''Hollywood Grind''. August 18, 2006.{{dead link|date=October 2014}}</ref>

== Military interests ==
Throughout his film career, Willis has depicted several military characters in films such as ''[[The Siege]]'', ''[[Hart's War]]'', ''[[Tears of the Sun]]'', ''[[Grindhouse (film)|Grindhouse]]'' and ''[[G.I. Joe: Retaliation]]''. Growing up in a [[military brat|military family]], Willis has publicly sold [[Girl Scout cookies]] for the United States armed forces. In 2002, Willis's second youngest daughter, Tallulah, suggested that he purchase Girl Scout cookies to send to troops. Willis purchased 12,000 boxes of cookies, and they were distributed to sailors aboard [[USS John F. Kennedy (CV-67)|USS ''John F. Kennedy'']] and other troops stationed throughout the Middle East at the time.<ref name="FindAr">{{cite news|last=Smith|first=Leah N.|title=Bruce Willis Moonlights as Off-Screen Hero with Cookie Donation|publisher=USS John F. Kennedy Public Affairs |url=http://www.navy.mil/submit/display.asp?story_id=1808 |accessdate=January 12, 2013 | date=May 29, 2002 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071118171431/http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_pnav/is_200205/ai_2244019612 <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = November 18, 2007}}</ref>[[File:ActorBruceWillis Iraq.jpg|thumb|left|Willis meets with Brigadier General [[Albert Bryant, Jr]] and deployed soldiers from the [[4th Infantry Division (United States)|4th Infantry Division]], in [[Tikrit]], Iraq, during his 2003 USO tour]] In 2003, Willis visited [[Iraq]] as part of the [[United Service Organizations|USO]] tour, singing to the troops with his band, The Accelerators.<ref name="CBSnews">{{cite news|last=Neal|first=Rome|title=Bruce Willis Sings For The Troops|publisher=[[CBS News]]|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2003/09/26/entertainment/main575209.shtml|date=September 26, 2003|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> Willis considered joining the military to help fight the second [[Iraq war]], but was deterred by his age.<ref name="RightOld">{{cite news|title=Hollywood's right reluctant to join Iraq debate|publisher=CNN|url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/ALLPOLITICS/03/07/iraq.celebs.reut/|date=March 7, 2003|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> It was believed he offered $1 million to any noncombatant who turns in terrorist leaders [[Osama bin Laden]], [[Ayman al-Zawahiri]], or [[Abu Musab al-Zarqawi]]; in the June 2007 issue of ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]'', however, he clarified that the statement was made hypothetically and not meant to be taken literally. Willis has also criticized the media for its coverage of the war, complaining that the press were more likely to focus on the negative aspects of the war:

<blockquote>I went to Iraq because what I saw when I was over there was soldiers—young kids for the most part—helping people in Iraq; helping getting the power turned back on, helping get hospitals open, helping get the water turned back on and you don't hear any of that on the news. You hear, <nowiki>'</nowiki>X number of people were killed today,<nowiki>'</nowiki> which I think does a huge disservice. It's like spitting on these young men and women who are over there fighting to help this country.<ref name="Holly">{{cite web|title=Willis Fights for Iraqi Troops |publisher=Hollywood.com |url=http://www.hollywood.com/news/Bruce_Willis_Fights_for_Iraqi_Troops_/2436202 |date=March 9, 2005|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref></blockquote>

Willis stated in 2005 that he wanted to "make a pro-war film in which American soldiers will be depicted as brave fighters for freedom and democracy."<ref name="TimesOnline">{{cite news|last=Baxter|first=Sarah|title=Bruce Willis comes out fighting for Iraq's forgotten GI heroes|work=The Times |location=London |url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/world/article597133.ece |date=November 27, 2005|accessdate=May 10, 2009 }}</ref> The film would follow members of [[1st Battalion, 24th Infantry Regiment|Deuce Four, the 1st Battalion, 24th Infantry]], who spent considerable time in [[Mosul]] and were decorated heavily for it. The film is to be based on the writings of blogger [[Michael Yon]], a former [[United States Army Special Forces]] soldier who was [[Embedded journalism|embedded]] with Deuce Four and sent regular dispatches about their activities. Willis described the plot of the film as "these guys who do what they are asked for very little money to defend and fight for what they consider to be freedom."<ref name="Holly3">{{cite web|title=Willis to Make Movie Honoring U.S. Troops in Iraq |publisher=Hollywood.com |url=http://www.hollywood.com/news/Willis_to_Make_Movie_Honoring_US_Troops_in_Iraq/3469589 |date=November 28, 2005|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref>

== Cultural references ==
In 1996, Roger Director, a writer and producer from ''Moonlighting'', wrote a [[roman à clef]] on Willis titled ''A Place to Fall''.<ref name="NYTimes">{{cite news |last=Gates |first=Anita |title=Moonlighting |work=The New York Times |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9A00E5DC1439F937A15750C0A960958260 |date=March 24, 1996|accessdate=May 10, 2009}}</ref> [[Cybill Shepherd]] wrote in her 2000 autobiography, ''Cybill Disobedience'', that Willis was angry at Director, because the character was written as a "neurotic, petulant actor." In 1998, Willis participated in ''[[Apocalypse (video game)|Apocalypse]]'', a [[PlayStation]] video game. The game was originally announced to feature Willis as a sidekick, not as the main character. The company reworked the game using Willis's likeness and voice and changed the game to use him as the main character.<ref name="EWeekly" />
